What is ICT?

ICT - Information and Communication Technology is a Senior High School strand that prepares you for a career in Information and Communication Technology. It equips you with advanced skills in computer systems, programming, creating web pages, and basic animation. In ICT Strand, you will acquire valuable skills in programming, allowing you to expand your knowledge in this area.


Overview of the ICT Strand

Information and Communication Technology or ICT Strand is one of the strands offered under Technical-Vocational Livelihood (TVL) Track of K to 12 curriculum. ICT strand subjects seek to teach students concepts and skills in information technology.

ICT in Senior High School equips students with the skills and knowledge they need to qualify for TESDA-backed certifications such as the Certificate of Competency (COC) and National Certifications (NC). The programs and practical experiences offered in the ICT Strand ensure that TVL-ICT students can apply for IT-related jobs right after SHS graduation.

Objectives of ICT Strand



The ICT strand in SHS is designed to equip senior high school students with the necessary technical skills to tackle the IT industry. Student enrolled in the ICT Strand are expected to:

  • Utilize industry standard equipment and to think critically and solve problems involving software, hardware, and other digital domains
  • Be confident and familiar with standard tech and software necessary to carry out tasks expected of them in order to contribute meaningfully to their respective teams
  • To apply their knowledge with decisiveness and experience when pursuing further education or applying oneself in a profession fresh out of senior high school


    • Advantages of ICT Strand



    Enrolling in the ICT strand for SHS gives Senior High School learners valuable knowledge that enables them to either dive immediately into technical professions befitting their interests and specializations, or to pursue further studies aligned with their track. Here are some valuable advantages of pursuing the ICT strand in SHS:

  • TVL-ICT graduates can confidently jump into the path of their chosen career knowing that their track has given them ample preparation — whether they want to build specializations by taking up further studies or they wish to begin working as soon as possible
  • Senior high school students in this track receive valuable experience in practical applications of their knowledge which they can maximize in both solo and team projects, shaping them valuable members of any workplace
  • Students can build a better appreciation of the advancing technology of today, while being exposed to opportunities that encourage them to think about how they can safeguard and contribute to one of the world’s most dynamic industries.


    • Possible College Courses Under the ICT Strand



  • Computer Engineering - Computer Engineering is a branch of engineering that weaves together computer sciences and electronic engineering in order to develop computer hardware and software.
  • Information Technology - Information Technology focuses on information science and is similar to computer science. Unlike the former, students do not create the hardware and software but rather, learn how to apply these innovations in real-world scenarios.
  • Software and Network Engineering - Software and Network Engineering revolves around the systematic development and application of software and network applications including browsers and mobile applications.
  • Multimedia Arts - Multimedia Arts is another course under the ICT strand. It teaches how to manipulate various art forms including text, graphics, sound, animation, graphics, and videos in order to create fresh designs. Multimedia artists can find work in advertising, the entertainment industry, or fine arts.
  • Digital Illustration and Animation - Digital Illustration and Animation engages students to create meaningful and thought-provoking content. It piques their imagination and encourages them to bring to life their creativity through storytelling, illustration, and 2D animation.
  • Graphic Arts - Graphic Arts is centered around motion graphics, animation, design theory, project management, and digital effects. Graduates of this program can successfully enter advertising, entertainment, business, and fine arts fields.
