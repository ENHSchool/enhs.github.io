Science Technology and Engineering (STE)


Junior high school Science, Technology, and Engineering (STE) refers to the educational curriculum and subjects taught to students in the middle school or junior high school level. This curriculum focuses on providing students with a foundational understanding of scientific concepts, technological principles, and engineering principles.


The STE curriculum is made to encourage problem-solving, critical thinking, inquiry-based learning, and practical experiences. It aims to improve students' technological know-how, engineering attitude, and scientific literacy. The following specific subjects are covered:



  • Science: Students learn about various branches of science, including biology, chemistry, physics, and earth science. They explore scientific concepts, conduct experiments, and learn about the scientific method.
  • Technology: Students gain an understanding of technology and its applications in different fields. They learn about digital literacy, computer programming, information technology, and may explore topics such as robotics, coding, and computer-aided design.
  • Engineering: Students are introduced to engineering principles, design processes, and problem-solving techniques. They learn about different engineering disciplines, such as civil engineering, mechanical engineering, and electrical engineering. Students may engage in hands-on projects, building prototypes, and solving engineering challenges.


The aim of the Junior High School STE curriculum is to provide students with a strong foundation in science, technology, and engineering, fostering their interest in these fields and preparing them for further study and career opportunities in related areas.
